Gen4Regen

Addressing Training Data Scarcity using Image Generators:
The Gen4Regen Dataset for Forest Regeneration Mapping

Abstract

Sustainable forest management relies on precise species composition mapping, yet traditional ground surveys are labour-intensive and geographically constrained. While Uncrewed Aerial Vehicles (UAVs) offer scalable data collection, the transition to deep learning-based interpretation is bottlenecked by the severe scarcity of expert-annotated imagery, particularly in complex, visually heterogeneous regeneration zones. This paper addresses the dual challenges of data scarcity and extreme class imbalance in the semantic segmentation of fine-grained forest regeneration species by providing a scalable framework that reduces reliance on manual photo-interpretation for high-resolution, millimetre-level overhead imagery. Our methodology integrates real- world data with synthetic generation, introducing WilDReF-Q-V2, an expansion of a natural forest dataset featuring 13 977 new unlabelled and 50 labelled images. Crucially, we leverage the vision-language Nano Banana Pro model to simultaneously generate 2101 high-fidelity images and their corresponding pixel-aligned semantic masks from prompts, forming the Gen4Regen dataset. We evaluate the orthogonality of manual labels, automated pseudo-labels, and AI-generated data using Mask2Former and DINOv2 architectures. Our results demonstrate that AI-generated data is highly complementary to real-world pseudo-labels, with a unified training yielding an F1 score improvement of over 15 % compared to purely supervised baselines. Ablation studies indicate that even small quantities of prompt-generated data significantly enhance the representation of rare species, some of which saw per-species gains of up to 30 %. We conclude that vision-language models can serve as agile data generators, effectively bootstrapping perception tasks for niche AI domains where expert labels are scarce or unavailable. This paradigm enables practitioners to bypass seasonal data collection constraints and develop robust, specialized ecological monitoring systems with minimal manual effort.
